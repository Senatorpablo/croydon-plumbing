Common Plumbing Emergencies & What to Do

Published 15 January 2025 · 8 min read

Introduction

Plumbing emergencies don't wait for convenient timing. A burst pipe at midnight, an overflowing toilet on a Sunday morning, or a gas leak during a dinner party — these are the situations that test any homeowner. Knowing what to do in the critical minutes before a professional plumber arrives can mean the difference between a minor inconvenience and thousands of pounds of water damage. This guide covers the most common plumbing emergencies Croydon homeowners face and the practical steps you should take immediately.

Burst Pipes

A burst pipe is perhaps the most dramatic plumbing emergency. It can release hundreds of litres of water per hour, rapidly flooding rooms, damaging floors, walls and electrical systems, and destroying personal belongings. In Croydon, burst pipes are most common during winter when temperatures drop below freezing, causing water inside pipes to expand and split the pipe wall.

Immediate steps:

  1. Turn off your main water stopcock immediately. In most Croydon homes, this is located under the kitchen sink, in a downstairs cupboard, or outside near the water meter. Make sure everyone in your household knows where it is before an emergency occurs.
  2. Open all cold taps to drain the remaining water from the system. This reduces the volume of water that can escape from the burst.
  3. Turn off your electricity at the consumer unit if water is near electrical outlets, appliances or wiring. Do not touch wet electrical items.
  4. Move furniture and belongings away from the affected area. Use towels and buckets to contain as much water as possible.
  5. Call a professional plumber immediately. Croydon Plumbing Solutions offers 24/7 emergency call-outs with a target response time of 60 minutes.

Blocked Drains & Toilets

A blocked drain or toilet can go from a slow annoyance to a health hazard extremely quickly. When wastewater can't flow away, it backs up into sinks, showers and toilets — bringing bacteria, unpleasant odours and the risk of flooding. Common causes in Croydon homes include cooking fat poured down kitchen sinks, wet wipes flushed down toilets, and tree root ingress into older clay drainage pipes prevalent in the area.

Immediate steps:

  1. Stop using the affected fixture immediately. Running more water will only make the blockage worse and increase the risk of overflow.
  2. For a blocked toilet, try a plunger first. Create a good seal and push firmly — the pressure can often dislodge soft blockages.
  3. Never use chemical drain cleaners as a first resort. They can damage pipes, especially older ones, and create hazardous fumes when mixed with other substances already in the drain.
  4. If plunging doesn't work, or if multiple drains are blocked simultaneously (indicating a main drain issue), call a professional. This often requires CCTV drain survey equipment and professional-grade rodding or high-pressure jetting.

Gas Leaks

A gas leak is the most dangerous plumbing emergency. Natural gas is highly flammable and can cause explosions, while carbon monoxide — produced when gas doesn't burn properly — is odourless and potentially lethal. If you suspect a gas leak, treat it as a life-threatening emergency.

Immediate steps:

  1. Do NOT operate any electrical switches, including lights. Do NOT use your phone inside the property. A tiny spark can ignite gas.
  2. Open windows and doors to ventilate the property if safe to do so.
  3. Turn off the gas supply at the meter if you can do so safely. The emergency control valve is typically next to your gas meter.
  4. Evacuate the property immediately. Get everyone out — including pets.
  5. Once outside and at a safe distance, call the National Gas Emergency Service on 0800 111 999. This is a free, 24-hour service.
  6. Do not re-enter the property until a Gas Safe registered engineer has confirmed it is safe.

Overflowing Toilet

An overflowing toilet is both unpleasant and a contamination risk. It usually happens when the drain is blocked and the cistern continues to fill the bowl. The water that overflows is not clean — it carries harmful bacteria.

Immediate steps:

  1. Remove the lid from the cistern and lift the float arm or close the flapper valve to stop water entering the bowl. If your toilet has a push-button mechanism, look for an isolation valve on the pipe behind the toilet.
  2. Mop up spilled water wearing rubber gloves. Disinfect all affected surfaces thoroughly.
  3. Attempt to clear the blockage with a plunger. If this fails, do not keep flushing — call a plumber.

No Hot Water or Heating

Losing hot water or central heating — especially during a Croydon winter — is more than just uncomfortable. If your boiler has stopped working, it could indicate a serious fault including a failed pump, leaking heat exchanger, or a carbon monoxide risk if the flue is compromised. Before assuming the worst, check these basics:

  • Is the boiler displaying an error code? Note it down — it helps the engineer diagnose the problem faster.
  • Is the gas supply working? Check other gas appliances.
  • Has the pilot light gone out? Some older boilers can be relit following the manufacturer's instructions. If it won't stay lit, there may be a thermocouple or gas valve fault.
  • Is the pressure gauge reading between 1 and 1.5 bar? Low pressure will shut down most modern combi boilers. You can repressurise via the filling loop if you know how.
  • If the boiler is making unusual noises (banging, gurgling, whistling), turn it off immediately and call a Gas Safe registered engineer.

Leaking Water Heater

A water heater or cylinder leak can be subtle — a small drip from a valve — or severe, with water pouring from the tank body. A leaking cylinder can flood a hot press or airing cupboard rapidly, damaging ceilings below. More concerning, a leak from the tank body often indicates internal corrosion, meaning the unit is nearing failure and could rupture.

Immediate steps:

  1. Turn off the water supply to the cylinder using the isolation valve on the cold feed pipe.
  2. Turn off the heating supply (boiler or immersion heater) to prevent the element running dry and burning out.
  3. Contain the leak with towels and buckets. If water is leaking through a ceiling, place a bucket beneath the drip and poke a small hole in the ceiling bulge to release water in a controlled way — this prevents the entire ceiling from collapsing.
  4. Call a plumber to assess whether the cylinder can be repaired or needs replacing.

Frozen Pipes

During cold snaps in Croydon, exposed pipes — particularly in lofts, garages and external walls — are at risk of freezing. A frozen pipe may not burst immediately, but as the ice expands inside, it can split the pipe. When the ice later thaws, you have a burst pipe on your hands.

Immediate steps:

  1. Turn off the water at the main stopcock.
  2. Open the tap fed by the frozen pipe so melting water can flow out.
  3. Thaw the pipe gently using a hot water bottle, hair dryer (on low) or warm towels. Never use a naked flame or heat gun — this can damage the pipe and is a fire risk.
  4. Start thawing from the tap end and work back toward the coldest section.
  5. If the pipe has already split, call an emergency plumber immediately.

Prevention Tips

Many plumbing emergencies are preventable with basic maintenance and preparation:

  • Know your stopcock location: Make sure every household member knows where the main water stopcock is and how to turn it off.
  • Insulate exposed pipes: Foam lagging on loft and garage pipes costs a few pounds and can prevent freeze-burst events that cost thousands.
  • Never pour fat down the drain: Let cooking oil cool and dispose of it in the bin. Fat solidifies in pipes and causes stubborn blockages.
  • Only flush human waste and toilet paper: Wet wipes, even "flushable" ones, are a leading cause of drain blockages across South London.
  • Schedule an annual boiler service: A Gas Safe registered service identifies developing faults before they become dangerous breakdowns.
  • Fix small leaks early: That dripping tap or weeping joint will not fix itself. Small leaks become big problems, and they waste significant amounts of water over time.

Conclusion

Plumbing emergencies are stressful, but preparation and quick action can dramatically reduce the damage. The single most important thing you can do is know where your stopcock is and how to shut off your water supply.
